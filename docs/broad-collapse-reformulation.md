# Broad Collapse Reformulation

## Positive Target

- Status: Proven. The positive target is no longer minimal-sector uniqueness.
- Status: Proven. The reformulated target is finite-family fixed-order collapse.
- Status: Conjectural. Given an explicit finite admitted primitive-family catalog and fixed truncation order `\Delta \le 4`, the free-fall local worldline EFT should reduce to a finite family-conditioned normal form under the explicitly allowed reduction rules.

## What The Reformulated Theorem Does Not Claim

- Status: Proven. It does not claim that the admitted primitive-family catalog is unique.
- Status: Proven. It does not claim that unsuppressed new primitive families fail to produce new survivors.
- Status: Proven. It does not claim that the smallest physically justified minimal sector has already been identified.

## Reformulated Research Burden

- Status: Conjectural. The remaining positive burden is to generalize from explicit family audits to a theorem for arbitrary explicit finite family catalogs within the MVP assumptions.
- Status: Conjectural. That burden is about family-conditioned finite collapse and conditional manifold reduction, not about proving that no further families can ever enter.
- Status: Conjectural. The broad program therefore lives or dies on finite-family closure and reduction, not on uniqueness of the minimal sector.

## Operational Consequence

- Status: Proven. Family-admission attacks now serve as classification inputs rather than as existential threats to the entire collapse program.
- Status: Proven. A new primitive family that yields a fresh survivor is evidence against uniqueness, but only evidence against broad collapse if it also destroys fixed-order finiteness or reduction.
