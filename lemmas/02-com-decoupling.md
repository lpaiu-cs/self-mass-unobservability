# Lemma 02: COM Decoupling

## Statement

Status: Imported from prior work. For a quasi-static compact body in an external vacuum field, internal self-subtraction does not generate a new monopole force on the center of mass.

## Imported Proof Sketch

1. Status: Imported from prior work. Summing the internal equations of motion over constituents cancels pairwise internal forces in the center-of-mass equation.
2. Status: Imported from prior work. The leading external coupling is the monopole term `-M Phi_ext`.
3. Status: Imported from prior work. The first body-shape correction is quadrupolar:

```math
L_{\rm int/ext}
=
-\frac{1}{2} Q^{ij} \partial_i \partial_j \Phi_{\rm ext},
```

so a spherical body still follows the test-body free-fall law through that order.

## Use In Theorem A

- Status: Imported from prior work. The theorem does not need to entertain "self-force by COM bookkeeping" as a loophole.
- Status: Imported from prior work. Any leading body-dependent free-fall effect must enter through explicit response coefficients or higher multipoles, not through failed cancellation of internal forces.
