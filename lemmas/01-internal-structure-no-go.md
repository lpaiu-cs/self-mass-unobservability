# Lemma 01: Internal-Structure No-Go

## Statement

Status: Imported from prior work. Literal self-unobservability cannot be implemented by removing self-gravity directly from the compact body's equilibrium equations while keeping the body otherwise self-bound.

## Imported Proof Sketch

1. Status: Imported from prior work. In the toy fixed-radius sphere, turning off self-gravity drives the central pressure, gravitational binding energy, and virial support to zero, so any finite-radius object would require external confinement.
2. Status: Imported from prior work. In the Newtonian `n = 1` polytrope with fixed equation of state, scaling the self-gravity coefficient by `alpha` gives

```math
R(\alpha) \propto \alpha^{-1/2},
\qquad
\rho_c(\alpha) \propto \alpha^{3/2},
\qquad
P_c(\alpha) \propto \alpha^3.
```

3. Status: Imported from prior work. The `alpha -> 0^+` limit therefore decompactifies the configuration rather than producing a new finite compact equilibrium.

## Use In Theorem A

- Status: Imported from prior work. The free-fall theorem cannot source body dependence by changing the internal structure equations themselves.
- Status: Imported from prior work. Any surviving anomaly must live in passive coupling to external fields, clock readout, or another explicitly stated loophole class.
